# Activity Prose Reference

Purpose: concise instructional intent for each ACT type, written for curriculum and authoring reference.

Reference basis:
- `central/ACTIVITY_DICTIONARY.md`
- `central/ACTIVITY_PROFILES.md`

---

## ACT-001 - Listen Only

Learner listens to modeled language without producing an answer.

What it accomplishes: input anchoring. It establishes sound-form familiarity and attention to target phrasing before discrimination or production tasks.

Best use: orientation, exposure, or preview of upcoming language chunks.

## ACT-002 - Stress Pattern Identification

Learner identifies which syllable carries stress in a spoken item.

What it accomplishes: prosodic awareness. It builds detection of English stress placement and reduces L1 transfer errors in rhythm and emphasis.

Best use: when learners must hear and classify stress before they are asked to produce it.

## ACT-003 - Minimal Pair Detection

Learner distinguishes between closely related sounds or words (for example, same/different or select-correct choice).

What it accomplishes: phonemic contrast control. It trains the ear to separate confusable sounds that change meaning.

Best use: when intelligibility issues come from sound confusions rather than grammar or vocabulary gaps.

## ACT-004 - Intonation Pattern Match

Learner matches heard intonation to the correct contour or category.

What it accomplishes: intonation recognition. It builds control over meaning carried by pitch movement (questioning, certainty, attitude).

Best use: when the lesson target is meaning-through-prosody rather than lexical recall.

## ACT-005 - Listen and Repeat

Learner listens, records repetition, and receives pronunciation-oriented performance evidence.

What it accomplishes: guided production transfer. It moves from perception into controlled spoken output with immediate comparison against the target.

Best use: pronunciation and fluency rehearsal tied to a specific model phrase.

## ACT-006 - Shadowing (Inactive)

Learner tracks and repeats a model utterance in close timing with the source audio.

What it accomplishes: fluency timing and prosodic mimicry. It strengthens rhythm, stress, and pacing control under high temporal pressure.

Best use: intensive pronunciation conditioning when the goal is timing alignment to native-like delivery.

Current status: inactive in current CMS lane.

## ACT-007 - Delayed Repetition (Inactive)

Learner listens, waits through a delay window, then reproduces the utterance.

What it accomplishes: short-horizon retention plus production. It pushes learners from echoing into recall-based speech output.

Best use: bridge stage between immediate imitation and freer spoken production.

Current status: inactive in current CMS lane.

## ACT-008 - Read Aloud (Inactive)

Learner reads displayed text aloud and receives pronunciation-related feedback.

What it accomplishes: orthography-to-speech transfer. It connects visual word recognition to accurate spoken realization.

Best use: controlled reading-to-pronunciation practice for fluency and articulation calibration.

Current status: inactive in current CMS lane.

## ACT-009 - Multiple Choice - Audio

Learner listens to an audio prompt and selects the correct option.

What it accomplishes: auditory comprehension with constrained decision-making. It reinforces sound-to-meaning mapping and listening discrimination.

Best use: listening checks where the learner should identify the correct interpretation from alternatives.

## ACT-010 - Multiple Choice - Text

Learner reads a text prompt and selects the correct answer from choices.

What it accomplishes: controlled comprehension and form selection. It verifies understanding of vocabulary or grammar targets with low production load.

Best use: quick diagnostic or reinforcement checks on explicit language targets.

## ACT-011 - True / False

Learner evaluates whether a statement is true or false.

What it accomplishes: fast concept verification. It confirms binary understanding of a claim, rule, or meaning relation.

Best use: low-friction comprehension gating before moving to harder production tasks.

## ACT-012 - Odd One Out

Learner identifies which item does not belong in a set.

What it accomplishes: categorical discrimination. It strengthens contrastive understanding by forcing classification against peer items.

Best use: vocabulary, sound, or pattern grouping checks where one distractor reveals misunderstanding.

## ACT-013 - Drag-and-Drop Match

Learner matches paired items across two sets.

What it accomplishes: association mapping. It builds durable links between forms, meanings, or structures through active pairing.

Best use: lexical matching, phrase-function pairing, or grammar correspondence drills.

## ACT-014 - Word Sort

Learner sorts words into labeled categories.

What it accomplishes: rule-based categorization. It helps learners infer and apply grouping logic across lexical or grammatical classes.

Best use: classification practice when the instructional target is pattern recognition, not recall alone.

## ACT-015 - Sentence Reconstruction

Learner reorders or assembles tokens into a correct sentence.

What it accomplishes: syntax assembly under constraints. It develops control of word order and structural sequencing.

Best use: grammar behavior practice where correct ordering is the primary learning objective.

## ACT-016 - Tense Sort

Learner sorts sentence cards into tense bins.

What it accomplishes: tense contrast control. It trains recognition of form-function relationships across tense categories.

Best use: tense discrimination and rule consolidation across multiple sentence exemplars.

## ACT-017 - Fill in the Blank - Typed

Learner types missing word(s) into inline sentence gaps.

What it accomplishes: constrained productive retrieval. It requires recall and accurate insertion within sentence context.

Best use: targeted production checks where spelling/form precision matters.

## ACT-018 - Fill in the Blank - Word Bank

Learner fills sentence gaps using options from a word bank.

What it accomplishes: scaffolded production with selection pressure. It supports retrieval while preserving contextual grammar constraints.

Best use: intermediate support stage before fully open typed production.

## ACT-019 - Error Correction

Learner identifies and corrects an error in a sentence.

What it accomplishes: monitoring and repair skill. It develops explicit error detection plus corrective output.

Best use: post-instruction consolidation when learners should diagnose and fix known target errors.

## ACT-020 - Speak and Score

Learner speaks a prompted response and receives scored or analyzed feedback against a target.

What it accomplishes: pronunciation-performance calibration. It links spoken output to measurable accuracy expectations.

Best use: guided speaking practice where objective performance feedback is required.

## ACT-021 - Speech Match

Learner compares spoken output against modeled audio in a constrained matching task.

What it accomplishes: model-to-production alignment. It reinforces acoustic and articulation similarity to target speech patterns.

Best use: pronunciation refinement when learners need close imitation against explicit options or models.

## ACT-022 - Open Production - Prompted

Learner gives an open spoken response to a prompt, typically guided by target keywords.

What it accomplishes: prompt-driven fluency expansion. It increases ability to produce relevant content beyond fixed templates.

Best use: semi-open speaking tasks where lexical targets must still appear in output.

## ACT-023 - NPC Dialogue Tree

Learner navigates branching dialogue turns with an avatar or NPC.

What it accomplishes: situated response selection and pragmatics. It trains turn-taking, context fit, and intent-appropriate language choices.

Best use: scenario-based communicative practice where conversational consequences matter.

## ACT-024 - Environmental Puzzle

Learner solves language-linked tasks embedded in a visual or interactive puzzle context.

What it accomplishes: contextual reasoning with language constraints. It integrates vocabulary/grammar cues with scene-based problem solving.

Best use: engagement-heavy consolidation where language must be applied inside a task environment.

## ACT-025 - Audio Sequence Ordering

Learner listens to clips and arranges them in the correct sequence.

What it accomplishes: temporal listening organization. It develops ordering logic across auditory events and discourse flow.

Best use: listening tasks focused on sequence comprehension and procedural ordering.

## ACT-026 - Open Discussion

Learner produces extended spoken output in response to a discussion prompt.

What it accomplishes: free-form communicative production. It supports fluency, coherence, and pragmatic expression over longer turns.

Best use: capstone speaking moments where learners synthesize target language in less constrained output.
